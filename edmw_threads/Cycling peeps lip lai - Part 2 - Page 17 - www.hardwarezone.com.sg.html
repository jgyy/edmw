android17 wrote:
bicycle noob here, got a question.

When I manually spin my front wheel, it can spin for a long time before stopping. However when spinning my rear wheel, it doesnt spin as long.

Is it normal for the rear wheel to not spin as well as the front?
The gist and to simply put, your front wheel hub only got ball bearings but your rear wheel got ratchet/pawls mechanism for engagement n freewheeling purposes. Therefore are alot of resistance in the rear hub internally.

If u use a silent hub (newer tech), there is much lesser resistance inside the rear hub because the the ratchet will be totally disengaged during freewheeling.

Overall race performance of the rear hub is mostly determined by the engagement, not the freewheeling. So, nothing much to worry if your rear wheel doesn't spin as long as the front wheel. Unless u freewheel half the time u r on your bike, then u may upgrade to silent hub to reduce the resistance during freewheeling.
